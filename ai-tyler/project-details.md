WaterLocks — QR-Based Commerce Platform
Project Overview
WaterLocks is a mobile commerce platform that allows vendors to sell physical items through secure QR-enabled locks without requiring direct interaction with the buyer.
Customers scan a QR code on the lock, complete a purchase through a web checkout page, and receive the lock combination to retrieve the item. The system tracks scans, purchases, and product performance while enabling vendors to manage inventory remotely.
I served as the lead engineer and later full owner of the platform, responsible for the mobile app, backend integrations, payment systems, and operational tooling.
My Role
Originally contracted as the developer, I was later offered partial ownership due to the amount of engineering work and product development involved. When the original team stepped away from the project, I assumed full ownership and ongoing maintenance.
My responsibilities included:
iOS application development
backend integrations
Stripe payments and vendor payouts
vendor onboarding flows
QR code generation and lock management
infrastructure and hosting
operational tooling and moderation systems
The project required both software engineering and operational decision-making, including sourcing hardware components such as locks, label printers, and weather-resistant QR code stickers.
Core Features Built
Vendor Commerce System
Vendors use the mobile app to manage physical locks containing products.
Capabilities include:
creating and editing lock listings
setting prices and product descriptions
tracking scans and purchases
monitoring product performance
receiving notifications for sales
Locks can be reorganized into sections to help vendors track which product types sell best.
QR-Based Purchase Flow
Each lock contains a QR code generated by the system.
Customer flow:
Customer scans QR code
Web purchase page opens
Customer completes payment
System validates the purchase
Lock combination is revealed
This enables fully unattended sales, allowing vendors to sell products without interacting with buyers.
Role-Based Account System
The platform supports multiple user types with different permissions.
Vendors
Primary users who:
own locks
manage products
receive payouts
configure subscriptions
Vendors must create a Stripe Connect account so the system can process payments and send payouts.
Employees
Employees can be linked to one or more vendor accounts.
Permissions are configurable and may include:
editing locks
unlocking locks
managing products
Buyers
Customers who purchase products through QR codes.
The system tracks buyer activity through Stripe metadata and internal records to support fraud detection and refund handling.
Admin Users
Admin users manage platform safety and operations.
Capabilities include:
reviewing refund requests
banning users
reviewing fraud reports
moderating disputes between buyers and vendors
This system was necessary because the platform handled third-party commerce transactions.
Admin Application
I built a separate internal iOS admin application used by platform moderators.
Admin features include:
viewing orders and sales activity
handling refund requests
reviewing fraud reports
banning vendors or buyers
generating reports
Refunds were manually reviewed because disputes could occur between buyers and vendors.
QR Code & Hardware Integration
The system uses Zebra label printers to generate durable QR code stickers for locks.
Engineering work included:
generating QR code data
encoding purchase metadata
formatting Zebra printer commands
ensuring the labels were weather-resistant and durable
The QR system had to work reliably in outdoor environments where labels could be exposed to weather and physical wear.
Payments & Subscriptions
WaterLocks uses a hybrid payment model.
Vendor Payments
Vendors receive payments through Stripe Connect Express accounts, allowing funds to be transferred directly to them.
Buyer Purchases
Customers purchase items through Stripe checkout flows accessed via the QR code purchase page.
Vendor Subscriptions
Vendors must maintain an active subscription for QR codes to function.
Subscription management includes:
account activation
failed payment handling
subscription enforcement

LDS Taste Buds — Social Dining Platform

LDS Taste Buds is a social dining platform that organizes users into curated dinner events where participants meet new people in structured group settings.
I was hired by one of the owners of Recommenu after they saw my engineering work, communication style, and reliability. The project required building a complete cross-platform mobile application and supporting backend systems.

Seating Algorithm
One of the most complex parts of the system was the automated seating system.
The platform organizes users into tables based on the event structure.
Seating Phases
The seating process operates in three phases:
Phase 1 — Early Buyers (12 hours before event)
Users who purchase tickets earlier are seated first. They receive notifications containing:
restaurant details
seating assignments
event information
Phase 2 — Late Buyers (12 hours to 30 minutes before event)
Users who purchase during this window are temporarily queued and seated closer to the event start time.
Phase 3 — Final Buyers (30 minutes before to 30 minutes after event)
Users who join during this period are seated immediately.
During this phase some seating constraints may relax to ensure users are not left without a table.
Seating Rules
The system attempts to enforce several rules:
avoid seating users alone when possible
respect blocked user relationships
preserve existing table assignments
prevent early users from being moved between tables
Manual seating tools exist for administrators to handle edge cases.
